How to Know If Your Child Needs a Tutor

Updated April 2025
Evan Varan

Is your child falling behind in school or losing interest in learning? These signs might mean it’s time to consider tutoring. In this article, we'll walk through the most common academic and behavioral indicators that suggest your child could benefit from one-on-one support.

1. Academic Red Flags

Common signs include declining grades, struggling with homework, difficulty in certain subjects, and inability to catch up after absences. If your child is constantly confused or spending hours on homework with little progress, it’s a red flag.

2. Emotional & Behavioral Changes

Frustration, avoidance, low confidence, or physical symptoms like stomachaches can all be reactions to school-related stress. These can be addressed with compassionate, targeted tutoring support.

3. Special Needs or Advanced Learners

Children with ADHD, dyslexia, or who are gifted often need personalized support—whether to catch up or to be challenged. Tutors can bridge these gaps with specialized approaches.

4. What a Tutor Can Do

Good tutors don’t just boost grades—they rebuild confidence, fill learning gaps, and teach study skills that last a lifetime.

5. How to Talk to Your Child About Tutoring

Present tutoring as a tool for growth. Let them feel in control of the decision, and explain that even top students benefit from support.
