Preparing for College Entrance Exams: A Parent and Student Guide

Updated April 2025
Evan Varan

Preparing for college entrance exams like the SAT or ACT can feel overwhelming for both students and parents. These tests play a significant role in college admissions, scholarships, and academic placement. With the right plan and support, students can build the skills and confidence they need to succeed. Here’s how to approach test prep with strategy and focus.

1. Understand the Exam Format

Start by learning the structure of the SAT or ACT. Know what subjects are tested, how much time is allowed per section, and how the scoring works. Understanding the format helps reduce anxiety and guides study priorities.

2. Start Early — Ideally by Sophomore or Junior Year

Beginning preparation early allows for steady, low-stress practice. Sophomore year is ideal for diagnostic testing, while junior year is prime for formal prep and testing. Waiting until senior year often leads to rushed preparation and missed opportunities.

3. Take a Diagnostic Practice Test

Practice exams reveal current skill levels, strengths, and areas for improvement. Use results to build a targeted study plan instead of wasting time on topics your child already understands well.

4. Create a Study Schedule

Consistency matters more than cramming. Block out regular weekly time for review, practice questions, and full-length mock exams. This builds endurance and reduces test-day burnout.

5. Prioritize Weak Areas

If your child struggles with reading comprehension, math timing, or grammar, spend more time there. Customized study is more efficient than general review.

6. Learn Test-Taking Strategies

Beyond content review, students should learn strategies like time management, educated guessing, and eliminating wrong answer choices. A tutor can teach these techniques in real test scenarios.

7. Track Progress Over Time

Retake practice tests every few weeks to monitor progress and adjust study goals. Improvement is motivating and helps refine test-day expectations.

8. Consider Professional Tutoring Support

Test prep tutors provide accountability, expert guidance, and emotional support. A good tutor knows the test inside out and can customize sessions to match your student’s learning style and college goals.

9. Don’t Forget About Self-Care

Sleep, nutrition, and mindset all play a role in performance. Encourage breaks, physical activity, and stress-management routines throughout the process.

Take the First Step Today

College entrance exams don’t have to be a source of stress. With a smart strategy and steady support, your child can approach test day with clarity and confidence. Schedule a test prep session today and give your child a proven path to college success.
